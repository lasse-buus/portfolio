★ Optional detour
Koyasan — Temple overnight

Rated the single most memorable experience by family who have visited Japan multiple times. A UNESCO mountain town at 850m with over 100 Buddhist temples. Stay in a shukubo (temple lodging), eat shojin ryori vegetarian cuisine, and join morning prayer at dawn.

How to get there: Nankai Railway from Namba → Gokurakubashi (~80 min) + cable car. Not covered by JR Pass (~¥1,500 pp each way).
If adding: Drop one Osaka night (3 → 2 nights) and spend 1 night on Koyasan.
Book early: Ekoin, Kongo Sanmaiin, or Fukuchiin — September fills up fast.
Map: https://maps.app.goo.gl/eZo5W5w3kEGXe9au7

🍣 Where to eat
Kanazawa food guide

Kanazawa is one of Japan's top food cities. September means the start of nodoguro (blackthroat seaperch) season — the city's prized fish, much cheaper here than elsewhere in Japan.

Lunch — Morimori Sushi (Omicho Market): Famous conveyor belt sushi. Hokuriku sweet shrimp, sea urchin, seasonal nigiri. Arrive 11:30am.

Lunch — TILE: Seafood rice bowl in a renovated 110-year-old machiya. ~30 toppings. Modern Japanese.

Dinner — Tachiguisushi Yusho: Hokuriku's first stand-up sushi bar, supervised by a 2-Michelin-star chef. Japan Restaurant Award "Best 100" 2025.

Dinner — Kourin Sushi: Since 1951. English menu. Closed Wed/Sun.

Must-try: Nodoguro · Kanazawa oden · Jibuni (duck stew) · Noto Beef · Ishikawa sake

Money & payments
💴 Cash

Always carry ¥10,000–20,000. Many temples, shrines, small restaurants, and markets are cash only. Withdraw at 7-Eleven or Post Office ATMs. Note: PokéPark is fully cashless.

💳 Visa / Mastercard

Accepted at all major hotels, department stores, and chain restaurants. Not reliable everywhere — always have cash backup.

🚃 Suica IC Card

Most useful thing to set up. Tap on all trains, buses, convenience stores, vending machines. iPhone: Welcome Suica app at home. Android: Google Pay.

🚄 JR Pass — buy in Norway

Covers all Shinkansen + Kanazawa Hokuriku line. Must buy outside Japan. 14-day ~¥50,000. Without it: ~¥55–60,000 pp. It pays for itself.

💸 Revolut / Wise

Better exchange rates than airport booths. Set up before departure, use to withdraw yen from ATMs.

⚠️ Koyasan note

Nankai Railway to Koyasan is NOT covered by JR Pass. Budget ~¥1,500 pp each way.

Apps to download before leaving
🗺 Google Maps

Download offline maps for Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka, Kanazawa before arrival.

🚄 Japan Travel by Navitime

Best for intercity trains. Shows JR Pass coverage, platform numbers, costs.

🔤 Google Translate

Download Japanese pack offline. Camera mode translates menus and signs — essential in Japan.

🎮 PokéPark KANTO app

Required for show lotteries, shop entry, ride slots. Available in English. Download before arrival and start lotteries at the Yomiuriland gate, not PokéPark itself.

🧳 Ecbo Cloak

Luggage storage at shops near stations. Invaluable when moving between cities.

📶 eSIM

Buy before departure (Airalo or Ubigi). Activate on arrival. Avoids Norwegian roaming charges.

Tips from family
🚲 Rent bikes in Kyoto

Best way to explore the temples at your own pace.

⏰ Go early to temples

Kyoto is much more crowded than 2015. Fushimi Inari at sunrise is magical.

♨️ Onsen every day

You will get completely addicted. Don't be shy — completely normal, everyone is naked, nobody cares.

📚 Secondhand markets

Always great finds in Tokyo. Search Google Maps on the day — Shimokitazawa & Harajuku.

🎰 Pachinko

Pop into one at least once. Completely overwhelming in the best way.

🏮 Koyasan is unmissable

Family rates it the single best experience from all Japan trips. Staying with monks in a mountain temple is unlike anything else.
