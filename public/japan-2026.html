★ Optional detour
Koyasan — Temple overnight

Rated the single most memorable experience by family who have visited Japan multiple times. A UNESCO mountain town at 850m altitude with over 100 Buddhist temples. Stay overnight in a shukubo (temple lodging), eat shojin ryori vegetarian temple cuisine, and join morning prayer at dawn. The approach through ancient cedar forests is breathtaking.

How to get there: Nankai Railway from Namba → Gokurakubashi (~80 min) + cable car (~5 min). Not covered by JR Pass (~¥1,500 pp each way).
If adding: Drop one Osaka night (3 → 2 nights) and spend 1 night on Koyasan.
Book early: Ekoin, Kongo Sanmaiin, or Fukuchiin — September fills up fast.
Map: https://maps.app.goo.gl/eZo5W5w3kEGXe9au7

Kanazawa
Sep 10 – Sep 12 · 2 nights

Japan's best-kept secret — samurai districts, geisha tea houses, one of the country's top three gardens, and a seafood scene that rivals Tokyo. Far fewer tourists than Kyoto, yet just as culturally rich. September means the start of nodoguro season — the city's prized fish.

🍣 Where to eat
Kanazawa food guide

Kanazawa is considered one of Japan's top food cities. The Sea of Japan brings exceptional seafood — September means the start of nodoguro (blackthroat seaperch) season, the city's prized fish.

Lunch — Morimori Sushi (Omicho Market): Famous conveyor belt sushi using the morning's market catch. Hokuriku sweet shrimp, sea urchin, seasonal nigiri at reasonable prices. Arrive 11:30am to beat the queue.

Lunch — TILE: Seafood rice bowl in a renovated 110-year-old machiya. ~30 toppings to choose from, beautifully presented. Modern Japanese.

Dinner — Tachiguisushi Yusho: Hokuriku's first stand-up sushi bar, supervised by the chef behind a 2-Michelin-star restaurant. Ranked Japan Restaurant Award "Best 100" 2025. Casual format, world-class fish.

Dinner — Kourin Sushi: Long-established (1951), English menu with photos, Kanazawa seafood sourced daily. Great sake from Ishikawa Prefecture. Closed Wed/Sun.

Must-try local dishes: Nodoguro (blackthroat seaperch) · Kanazawa oden · Jibuni (duck stew) · Noto Beef · Local Ishikawa sake

Practical Info

Everything you need to know before you leave Oslo.

Money & payments
💴 Cash

Always carry ¥10,000–20,000. Many temples, shrines, small restaurants, and markets are cash only. Withdraw at 7-Eleven or Post Office ATMs on arrival.

💳 Visa / Mastercard

Accepted at all major hotels, department stores, and chain restaurants in cities. Not reliable everywhere — always have cash backup.

🚃 Suica IC Card

The most useful thing to set up. Tap on/off all trains, buses, subways, convenience stores, vending machines. iPhone: download Welcome Suica app at home. Android: add via Google Pay.

💴 JR Pass — buy in Norway

Covers all Shinkansen legs including Kanazawa (Hokuriku Shinkansen). Must buy outside Japan. 14-day pass ~¥50,000. Without it: ~¥55–60,000 pp just for trains. It pays for itself.

💸 Revolut / Wise

Better exchange rates than airport booths. Set up before departure, use to withdraw yen from ATMs.

⚠️ Koyasan note

Nankai Railway to Koyasan is NOT covered by JR Pass. Budget ~¥1,500 pp each way.

Apps to download before leaving
🗺 Google Maps

Download offline maps for Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka, Kanazawa before arrival. Essential for walking.

🚄 Japan Travel by Navitime

Best for intercity train routes. Shows JR Pass coverage, platform numbers, estimated costs. Better than Google Maps for trains.

🔤 Google Translate

Download the Japanese language pack offline. Camera mode translates menus and signs in real time — essential in Japan where much is in Japanese only.

🧳 Ecbo Cloak

Book luggage storage at shops and cafés near stations. Invaluable when moving between cities.

📶 eSIM

Buy a Japan data eSIM before departure (Airalo or Ubigi). Activate on arrival. Avoids Norwegian roaming charges. Alternative: buy a SIM at the airport.

Tips from family
🚲 Rent bikes in Kyoto

Best way to explore the temples at your own pace. Shounsai has free bikes.

⏰ Go early to temples

Kyoto is much more crowded than it was in 2015. Fushimi Inari at sunrise is magical.

♨️ Onsen every day

You will get completely addicted. Don't be shy — it's completely normal, everyone is naked, nobody cares.

📚 Secondhand markets

Always great finds in Tokyo. Search Google Maps on the day — best areas: Shimokitazawa, Harajuku.

🎰 Pachinko

Pop into a pachinko hall at least once. Completely overwhelming in the best way.

🏮 Koyasan is unmissable

Family rates it the single best experience from all Japan trips. Staying with monks in a mountain temple is unlike anything else.
